# Q. What does "churn" mean in this business context(A customer who cancelled their subscription or stopped ordering)

ans- In such a situation the following two can be the reason:
1. Voluntary churn: where a customer has stopped their subscription or stopped ordering becuase of dissatisfaction or
bad experience or a competitior offering a better offering.

2. Involuntary churn: when a customer stopped ordering or subscription due to payment failure or delivery issue or some other 
factor not necessarily voluntary.

# Q. What is the cost of churn? (Lost recurring revenue, higher CAC to replace them)

ans- The cost of churn is bigger than most beginners realize. It has three layers, and understanding all three is what makes 
your recommendation credible to leadership.

* Layer 1: Lost Recurring Revenue
Every churned customer takes their future orders with them. If the average customer spends ₹500 per month and 100 customers churn,
that is ₹50,000 in monthly revenue gone. This is the number most people stop at, but it is only the starting point.

* Layer 2: Lost Upsell Opportunity
An existing customer has a 65% probability of being upsold a higher-value product. A new prospect has only a 13% chance. So every 
churned customer also kills a future upsell opportunity the business was likely to close. That number needs to go into your cost 
estimate.

* Layer 3: Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
This is the most painful layer. The business now has to spend money to replace the customer it already had. Studies show it costs 
5x more to acquire a new customer than to retain an existing one. So if churn jumped from 8% to 14% in your dataset, the business 
is not just losing revenue; it is being forced to spend significantly more on marketing and sales just to stay flat.

# Q. What would a useful answer look like? (A specfic segment + a speci c cause + one action)
ans: A useful answer has three parts.

A specific segment.
A specific cause.
One action.

So your answer should sound like this:
Older customers in Germany with low tenure and no active membership are the highest risk segment. Their churn is linked to low engagement and product usage. The bank 
should launch a retention offer for inactive customers in Germany within their first 6 months.

***Hypothesis*** 

1. Customers  with low tenure + low satisfaction have a higher chance of getting churned
    Why test: short relationship and poor experience together point to onboarding or product fit problems.
    Metrics to check: churn rate, median tenure, average satisfaction, complaint rate, day since last order.

    Primary metric - Churn rate 
    Secondary metric - Median tenure for segment, Avg satisfaction score, segment size
    Support metric - complaint rate, days since last order, order count, coupon used

2. Customers with low app hourse + low satisfaction are more likely to churn
    Why test: low engagement combined with low satisfaction points to poor app experience or irrelevant content.
    Metrics to check: churn rate, hour spend on app, satisfaction score, day since last order, prefered order category.

    Primary metric - churn rate
    Secondary metric - Avg hours spend on app, median satisfation score, segent engagement rate
    Support metric - days since last orfer, preferred order category, complaint rate, order count

3. Customers with low tenure + low order count are likely to churn
    Why test: new customers who never convert to repeat buyers likely face value or trust issues.
    Metrics to check: churn rate, order count distribution, coupon used, order amount hike from last year, day since last order.

    Primary metric - Churn rate
    Secondary metric - Distribution of order count (mean, median), segment repeat rate, coupon usuage rate
    Support metric - order amount hike from last year, days sine last order, Avg orders value

4. Customers with high device count + low satisfaction are more likely to churn
    Why test: many registered devices plus low satisfaction suggests cross‑device UX or fraud/notification problems.
    Metrics to check: churn rate, number of devices, satisfaction score, preferred login device, complaint rate.

    Primary metric - Churn rate 
    Secondary metric - Avg numbers of registered revices, avg satisfacition score, share of segment using multiple login devices
    Support metric -preferred login device distribution, complaint rate, hours spend on app

5. Customers with high days since last order + low order count
    Why test: customers who rarely order and have long inactivity windows likely churn from attrition not price.
    Metrics to check: churn rate, day since last order, order count, cashback amount, coupon used.

    Primary metric - Churn rate
    Secondary metric - median days since last order, Avg order count, segment inactivity share
    Support metric - cashback amount, coupon used, order amount hike from last year.
