## Phase 5 Conclusion

    This project identified the main churn problem in a subscription ecommerce business and turned it into one clear retention action.

    1. The analysis showed that churn is concentrated in early lifecycle customers. The strongest signals were low tenure, low satisfaction, complaint behavior, 
    low order count, and longer gaps since the last order. The balanced logistic model also confirmed that the model should prioritize finding churned customers, even 
    at the cost of some false alarms.

    2. The cluster analysis gave the clearest business picture. Cluster 0 had the highest churn rate at 36 percent, the lowest satisfaction score, low order depth, 
    and the highest complaint level. Cluster 1 was the loyal group with the lowest churn rate at 8 percent and the strongest engagement. Cluster 2 was the largest middle 
    group with moderate risk.

    3. The root cause is weak early customer experience. Customers who do not build habit, do not place repeat orders, and face complaints early are much more likely to 
    churn. Tenure protects against churn, which means the business must win retention early, before customers drop off.

    4. The best targeted action is a first 30 day onboarding and recovery program for Cluster 0. This should trigger for customers with low tenure and low satisfaction, 
    add fast complaint resolution, and send a small incentive for the next order. The goal is to improve early experience and convert first time buyers into repeat customers.

    5. Final leadership takeaway: the business should stop treating churn as a broad problem. It should focus on new, dissatisfied customers with complaints, because they 
    represent the highest risk and the clearest retention opportunity.
